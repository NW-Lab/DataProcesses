# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We commit to making participation in DataProcesses a respectful, harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of identity, background, experience, ability, appearance, or viewpoint. Technical disagreement is welcome; personal attacks and exclusionary behavior are not.

## Expected behavior

Participants should communicate with empathy, assume good intent while addressing harmful impact, give actionable technical feedback, respect differing experience levels, and acknowledge the work of others. Decisions should focus on the project’s users, maintainability, safety, and documented goals.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, threats, discriminatory language, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation, doxxing, sustained disruption, and publishing another person’s private information are unacceptable. Bad-faith use of project communication channels, including repeated off-topic demands or attempts to bypass moderation, is also unacceptable.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, project events, and other spaces where someone is representing DataProcesses.

## Reporting and enforcement

Do not post sensitive conduct reports publicly. Contact the repository maintainers through a private channel listed on the repository owner’s GitHub profile. If the report concerns a GitHub-hosted interaction and no private maintainer channel is available, use GitHub’s platform reporting tools. Maintainers will review reports as promptly and impartially as practical, preserve confidentiality where possible, and may remove content, issue warnings, temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants according to severity and history.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this code in good faith may be removed from moderation responsibilities by the project’s other maintainers.

## Attribution

This policy is informed by the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/) and adapted for the current early-stage structure of DataProcesses.
